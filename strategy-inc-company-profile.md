## Executive summary

**Strategy Inc.** is a U.S. public company that combines:

1. **Bitcoin treasury strategy** — large corporate holdings of bitcoin (BTC), funded partly through equity and debt/preferred capital markets. The company markets itself as the world’s first and largest **Bitcoin Treasury Company**.
2. **Enterprise software** — analytics / business intelligence (BI) and AI-powered enterprise software sold to organizations globally (legacy MicroStrategy product line).

For partners, vendors, or analysts, the company is relevant in **digital assets**, **capital markets / structured equity**, and **enterprise analytics** — with outsized public attention on BTC holdings and related securities.

## Business model

### 1. Bitcoin treasury & capital markets

- **Holdings:** Company accumulates BTC using proceeds from ATM equity sales, debt/preferred offerings, and operating cash flow (when applicable).
- **Narrative:** Increase **bitcoin per share** (company KPI: **BPS** — Bitcoin Per Share in “sats”) faster than diluted share count grows.
- **Instruments:** Issues multiple security types so investors can choose different exposure to BTC and dividend/credit profiles (e.g. **STRC** as a flagship “digital credit” preferred).
- **Disclosure:** Publishes a **Strategy Dashboard** on its website for BTC purchases, holdings, and selected KPIs (company describes this as a Regulation FD disclosure channel).

### 2. Enterprise software

- **Product area:** Cloud-native, AI-powered **enterprise analytics** / BI platform.
- **Customers:** Global enterprises (thousands of customers cited in company materials).
- **Brand line:** “Intelligence Everywhere™” (trademark per company).
- **Revenue:** Software and related services remain a **smaller dollar story** than balance-sheet BTC and capital-markets activity, but it is the operating business with recurring revenue characteristics.

## Key company-defined KPIs (for reading their IR)

| KPI | What it measures (simplified) |
|-----|------------------------------|
| **BPS** | BTC holdings ÷ assumed fully diluted shares (in satoshis per share) |
| **BTC Yield** | % change in BPS over a period |
| **BTC Gain** | Absolute BTC amount implied by BTC Yield |
| **BTC $ Gain** | BTC Gain × market price of BTC at period end |

These are **not** traditional “dividend yield” or “EPS growth.” The company’s filings explain limitations (e.g. senior claims of debt/preferred, liquidation priority, mark-to-market vs. KPIs).

## Risks to note (business / counterparty view)

1. **BTC price volatility** — Balance sheet and reported earnings swing with bitcoin market price.
2. **Dilution & capital structure** — Multiple share classes and convertibles/preferreds; common equity sits junior to many claims.
3. **Regulatory & accounting** — Crypto asset accounting and disclosure rules evolve; tax treatment of distributions on preferreds is complex (company discusses return-of-capital expectations for U.S. holders — shareholders must use own tax advisors).
4. **Concentration** — Business identity and market valuation are tightly linked to BTC strategy execution.
5. **Operating business scale** — Software revenue is modest relative to asset base; do not assume software alone supports enterprise value.
