## Who he is (one line)

**Warren Edward Buffett** (born 30 August 1930) is **Chairman** of **Berkshire Hathaway** (NYSE: BRK.A / BRK.B), the diversified holding company through which most of his wealth is held. He stepped down as **CEO** at the end of **2025** (succession to **Greg Abel**); he remains **Chairman** as of early 2026 per public reporting.

## Famous quotes (verified or well-sourced)

### 1. Reputation (the one you asked about)

> **It takes 20 years to build a reputation and five minutes to ruin it. If you think about that, you'll do things differently.**

**Attribution note:** This line is **widely attributed** to Buffett online and in business books. [Wikiquote](https://en.wikiquote.org/wiki/Warren_Buffett) traces an early appearance to **Dan Robert Anderson**, *Corporate Survival: The Critical Importance of Sustainability Risk Management* (2005), p. 138, **as quoted** there — i.e. secondary sourcing, not a Berkshire shareholder letter. Treat as **popular wisdom associated with Buffett**; for academic citation, track down Anderson or an original Buffett speech if required.

---

### 2. Fear and greed (markets)

> **You want to be greedy when others are fearful. You want to be fearful when others are greedy. It's that simple.**

- Often cited from a **Charlie Rose** interview, **PBS**, **1 October 2008** (financial crisis period). See [Wikiquote — Buffett](https://en.wikiquote.org/wiki/Warren_Buffett) for links.

A related line in **Berkshire shareholder letters** (value vs. price / patience):

> **Investors should remember that excitement and expenses are their enemies. And if they insist on trying to time their participation in equities, they should try to be fearful when others are greedy and greedy only when others are fearful.**

- [2004 Chairman’s letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2004ltr.pdf) (PDF on berkshirehathaway.com).

---

### 3. Swimming naked (risk / leverage)

> **After all, you only find out who is swimming naked when the tide goes out.**

- **Berkshire Hathaway Chairman’s Letter** (famously **1992** letter; still quoted in later letters). Primary: [Berkshire annual letters archive](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html).

---

### 4. Price vs. value

> **Long ago, Ben Graham taught me that “Price is what you pay; value is what you get.” Whether we're talking about socks or stocks, I like buying quality merchandise when it is marked down.**

- **2008 Chairman’s letter** to Berkshire shareholders. [Letters archive](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html).

---

### 5. Holding period

> **In fact, when we own portions of outstanding businesses with outstanding managements, our favorite holding period is forever.**

- Appears in **Berkshire shareholder letters** (e.g. discussed in **1988** letter context in collections). Primary: [Annual letters](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html).

---

### 6. Circle of competence (understanding what you do)

> **There's a whole bunch of things I don't know a thing about — I just stay away from those. So, I stay within what I call my circle of competence.**

- **1996 Berkshire Hathaway annual meeting**, as summarized/quoted in secondary sources; see [Wikiquote — Buffett](https://en.wikiquote.org/wiki/Warren_Buffett).

**Business takeaway:** The disciplined idea is **not** to trade complexity for ego — stay where you can judge **economic reality**.

---

### 7. Derivatives — “financial weapons of mass destruction”

From the **2002** Chairman’s letter (Buffett and Munger’s warning on derivatives; wording established in primary PDF):

> **The derivatives genie is now well out of the bottle, and these instruments will almost certainly multiply in variety and number until some event makes their toxicity clear. … In our view, however, derivatives are financial weapons of mass destruction, carrying dangers that, while now latent, are potentially lethal.**

- Primary: [Berkshire 2002 annual report / letter PDF](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2002pdf.pdf) (berkshirehathaway.com).

---

### 8. Kindness of strangers (liquidity / leverage)

> **We never want to count on the kindness of strangers in order to meet tomorrow's obligations.**

- **2008 Chairman’s letter**. [Letters archive](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html).

---

### 9. Mr. Market (Ben Graham, via Buffett)

Not Buffett’s coinage — **Benjamin Graham’s** allegory — but central to Buffett’s teaching: the market is a **partner** offering you a price every day; you may accept or ignore it. Explained in many **Berkshire letters** (e.g. **1987** letter discussion). [Letters](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html).

---

### 10. Philanthropy (Giving Pledge)

Buffett’s written pledge to donate the overwhelming majority of wealth — primary document:

- [“My Philanthropic Pledge” (PDF)](https://givingpledge.org/pdf/letters/Buffett_Letter.pdf) on [The Giving Pledge](https://givingpledge.org).

## About the line: *“Those who truly understand it wouldn’t do it”*

That exact sentence is **often repeated** in social media and slide decks as if Buffett said it about **Wall Street products**, **derivatives**, or **complex trades**.

- There is **no widely accepted primary source** (e.g. a specific Berkshire letter) that uses **that exact wording** as a Buffett canonical quote.
- **Close verified themes from Buffett/Munger:**  
  - **Circle of competence** — avoid what you cannot judge.  
  - **Derivatives danger** — **2002 letter** (“financial weapons of mass destruction”).  
  - **2001 shareholder meeting** discussion (General Re / derivatives): Buffett said complex derivatives books are **hard to understand** and suggested many **CEOs** of such businesses **may not fully understand** them — a warning about **opacity and incentives**, not a one-liner meme.

For business use, prefer the **verified** lines in sections **6** and **7** above.
